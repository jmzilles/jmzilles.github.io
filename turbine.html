This 3D printed scale model of a wind turbine stemmed from the final project of Engineering 26, a 3D modeling class at the University of California, Berkeley. My team and I were tasked with designing a blade and base for a wind turbine which would produce maximum energy in a wind tunnel and sustain a maximum pulling force. The blade and base were to be printed out of ABS and we were given strict size regulations.

My team and I researched air foils and utilized Solidworks to create a three bladed turbine design which would theoretically produce the maximum energy output based on the conditions. We designed the base by imputing our maximum dimensions into Solidworks and using finite element analysis to achieve the strongest design based on the expected force. These models were 3D printed in ABS and glued together. When tested, we discovered mistakes in our angling of the blades, but proved that FEA was the superior method for base design as we topped our class's leaderboard for strength, being unable to break our base with all of the weights provided.
