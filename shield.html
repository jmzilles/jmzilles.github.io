COVID Face Shields

At the beginning of the COVID-19 crisis, there was a rush to provide PPE to hospitals that were overwhelmed with patients and didn't have the proper safety equiptment for healthcare workers. 3D printing face shields became a way for community members to help hospitals protect their workers. I worked with one of my middle school teachers, Alex Wulff, and some other ex-students of his to help mass produce face shields. We printed out standardized headband files, then fastened elastic bands and plastic shields to them. The images below show a single batch of face shields that I printed. All in all, we donated hundreds of face shields, some going to oour local hospitals, some being shipped to other hospitals, including internationally. While I do not overestimate the impact my shields made, I am proud that I was able to find a way to help protect the people putting themselves on the front line during this crisis.
